Child Assent to Participate in Research

Study Title for Participants: Decision making with rewards and risks in youth

Formal Study Title: Development of multi-dimensional decision making in typically developing and trauma-exposed youth (DIMENSION)

Lead Researcher: Amy Cochran, (608) 262-0772, 610 Walnut St, Madison, WI

Institution: University of Wisconsin

We are trying to learn how young people make decisions that involve both risks and rewards. Understanding this type of decision-making may inform strategies to promote better mental health outcomes for youth.

We are asking you to participate in this study because you are between the ages of 11 and 17. We aim to include 30 young people like you in this research.

If you agree to participate in this study, you will:

  • Answer questions about yourself and your feelings.
  • Play a decision-making game involving both rewards and risks.
  • Provide feedback on how easy it was to understand and play the game.

The study will take about 30 minutes and can be completed entirely online. While there may be no direct benefits to you, your participation may help us learn things about decision making that could benefit other young people in the future.

Your responses will be anonymous. However, we may ask you sensitive questions about your feelings and you may see images of faces of people who look sad, angry, or fearful. Like any research, there may be unforeseen risks.

If you agree to take part in this research study, we will provide you a $10 gift card. You can choose from several companies (e.g., Apple, Starbucks, eBay).

Both you and your parent or guardian need must agree to your participation. It is okay for you to decide not to participate, without any consequences. If you choose not to participate, simply close this window. Should you have any additional questions, you may email the research team at seqer@math.wisc.edu.

If you check the box below and click “Agree,” it means that you have read this permission form and agree to be in this study.
